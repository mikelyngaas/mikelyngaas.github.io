Learnings from 30 days of building Town

July 8, 2026

I’m 30ish days into my time at Town, where we’re building an AI assistant for work. A few non-obvious learnings from building that assistant (with a stellar team!) thus far:

[1] We are still in the 1st inning of AI adoption at work.

It’s really easy to feel behind in the SF echo chamber, but the vast majority of people outside of Eng/Product/Design are still waiting for their “aha” moment with AI at work. It’s energizing and humbling to try to give it to them.

[2] User empathy has never been more important.

A superpower in AI product dev is to think like a non-AI pilled person: they don’t fundamentally care about skills, MCPs, or evals, they just want to work more efficiently across their everyday tools. It’s surprisingly hard sometimes for teams who live and breathe Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor.

[3] Related: 99.99% of people do not want to build their own AI chief of staff from scratch.

OpenClaw, Hermes, and the like are incredibly powerful, but despite what you see on Tech Twitter and LinkedIn, most people do not want to build (and maintain!) these stacks. They want a really strong team to do it for them.

[4] Building simple products is even harder when the barrier to building is so low.

It’s easy to say “just make it simpler” but I’m often reminded of Ryo Lu's essay[*] on this topic. Paraphrasing: to really make something simple, you have to deeply understand the whole system and its component parts, then rearrange them, surfacing only the absolutely needed abstraction to the user. Building Town is a crash course in iterating towards this!

[5] People are hitting their breaking point with LLM drivel[**]

You know: “genuinely,” “that’s not X, it’s Y”. It’s pervasive, and drives a lot of well-deserved mistrust of AI tooling. Town is not perfect here, and it’s all our jobs to build more authenticity into our experiences.

[*] Ryo’s essay: https://x.com/ryolu_/status/1990057444253241545 ↩

[**] Not one word of this post was AI-generated :) ↩
